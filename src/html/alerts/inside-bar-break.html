About Inside Bar Break

Another favourite alert that many day-traders and swing-traders alike use as an entry signal to get into low-risk, high-reward trades early to then ride the wave of follow through that often comes after an inside bar break.

Inside bars are formed when the market is in an equilibrium pattern on a shorter timeframe. Back on the larger timeframes, this type of price action is usually shown as a series of bars where each new bar fits completely within the high/low range of the preceding bar. Hence the term 'Inside Bars'.

The longer a chart has been within an equilibrium, tightening up as the range of the price action becomes smaller and smaller as time goes on, and thus forming more and more inside bars, the stronger the breakout move tends to be once it happens. The analogy often used by traders is that of a coiled up spring which gets compressed so much that when it is finally released, the move can often be explosive.

While many traders anticipate continuation in the direction of the preceding trend once the pattern breaks, the reality is that it is hard to predict which way equilibriums and inside bars end up breaking, so entering too early could lead to losses.

The inside bar alert makes it easy to be patient and wait for confirmation of direction. As soon as the price action starts to go outside of the range of the previous inside bar, the alert fires to notify you of this by giving you either a bullish or bearish signal.
