About Golden/Death Cross

A Golden Cross occurs when the 50 period Simple Moving Average (SMA) crosses above the 200 period SMA. This can indicate a long-term bull market going forward, especially when the crossover is accompanied by an increase in bullish trading volume. Once the Golden Cross occurs, the long-term moving average is considered a major support level.

A Death Cross is the opposite and occurs when the 50 period Simple Moving Average (SMA) crosses below the 200 period SMA. This can indicate a long-term bear market going forward, especially when the crossover is accompanied by an increase in bearish trading volume. Once the Death Cross occurs, the long-term moving average is considered a major resistance level.

It is important to note that the Golden / Death Cross signals are often lagging in nature. This is due to the length of the SMAs used to generate the crossovers. Traders would be wise to find additional confluence with other indicators such as the MACD or Abnormal Volume to determine if the signal should be considered reliable before basing trades on it. Nonetheless, they are used by many traders as the ultimate confirmation of a longer term change in market conditions from either bull market to bear market or vice versa.
